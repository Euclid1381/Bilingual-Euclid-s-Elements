S		 Book II   Proposition 8
And, since
    CG is equal to GQ, and QR to RP,
    AG is also equal to MQ, and QL to RF, [I.36]

But
    MQ is equal to QL, for they are complements of the parallelogram ML; [I.43] therefore
    AG is also equal to RF;
therefore
    the four areas AG, MQ, QL, RF are equal to one another.

Therefore
    the four are quadruple of AG.
