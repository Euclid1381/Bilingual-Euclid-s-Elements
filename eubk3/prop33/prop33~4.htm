S		 Book III   Proposition 33
Since then
    a straight line AD touches the circle ABE, and from the point of contact at A a straight line AB is drawn across the circle ABE,
    the angle DAB is equal to the angle AEB in the alternate segment of the circle. [III.32]
But
    the angle DAB is equal to the angle at C;
therefore
    the angle at C is also equal to the angle AEB.

Therefore
    on the given line segment AB the segment AEB of a circle has been described admitting the angle AEB equal to the given angle, the angle at C.

Being what it was required to do.
